# KP CORE PRINCIPLE

**The Three-Layer System**: Every planet and house cusp is identified by three overlapping lords:
1. **Sign Lord** (Rashi lord) — broadest domain
2. **Star Lord** (Nakshatra lord) — channels and colors the result
3. **Sub Lord** — the **final decision-maker**: YES the event will happen, or NO it will not

> *"A planet gives the results of its Star Lord's house connections more strongly than the houses it owns."*

**The Two Rules That Override Everything**:
- The **cuspal sub-lord** of a house determines whether that house's promise is fulfilled or denied
- A planet is strong as a significator when it operates in Dasha AND is a Ruling Planet at the time of judgment

# PART 5 — KP SUB-LORD THEORY

## The 249 Sub-Divisions

The zodiac (360°) → 27 nakshatras → each nakshatra divided into 9 sub-lords proportional to Vimshottari Dasha years. Starting from the nakshatra's own star lord and cycling through the Vimshottari sequence. Boundary overlaps create 249 unique position identifiers (not exactly 243 = 27×9).

## The Golden Rule

> *"The Sub-Lord is the final judge — it decides YES or NO for whether a house's promise materializes."*

**How to apply**:
1. Find the cuspal sub-lord of the relevant house (e.g., 7H for marriage)
2. List all houses the sub-lord signifies (via its star lord's connections)
3. If those houses include the **supporting houses** for the event → promise exists
4. If those houses include **denial houses** → event is blocked or delayed

## Sub-Lord of Sub-Lord (SSL)

For borderline cases: examine the sub-lord OF the sub-lord. If SSL confirms what the sub-lord indicates, confidence is maximum.

# PART 6 — SIGNIFICATOR METHODOLOGY

## The Four-Level Hierarchy (Strongest → Weakest)

| Level | Description | Strength |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **A** | Planets in the star of a planet **occupying** the house | Strongest |
| **B** | Planets **physically in** the house | 2nd |
| **C** | Planets in the star of the **house lord** | 3rd |
| **D** | The **house lord** itself | Weakest |

**Key principle**: "A planet gives the results of its star lord's house connections more strongly than the houses it owns."

**Empty house rule**: If no planets occupy a house → Levels A and B are absent. Only C and D apply.

**Own-star rule**: A planet in its own nakshatra is automatically the primary significator of its house of occupancy.

**Rahu/Ketu rule**: Additionally signify the houses of the planets they conjoin OR the sign lord of their occupied sign.

## Building a Significator Table

1. List all 12 houses with their cusp degrees (Placidus)
2. Note which planets physically occupy each house → **Level B**
3. For each occupant: find its nakshatra → all planets in THAT star lord's nakshatra → **Level A** for the occupied house
4. Identify each house's sign lord → **Level D**
5. Find the sign lord's nakshatra → all planets in THAT nakshatra → **Level C** for the house
6. Apply Rahu/Ketu extension rule
7. For empty houses: skip A and B; use only C and D

## Using the Table for Prediction

1. Identify relevant houses for the event (e.g., 2-7-11 for marriage)
2. Confirm cuspal sub-lord of primary house (7H) signifies supporting houses → promise check
3. Collect all Level A/B/C/D significators of the supporting houses
4. Eliminate retrograde significators (or flag for delay)
5. Note combust planets (weakened delivery)
6. Cross-reference with Ruling Planets at time of judgment
7. Find the Dasha/Bhukti/Antara where operative significators run

# PART 7 — RULING PLANETS (RP) TECHNIQUE

The 5 Ruling Planets are calculated at the **Time of Judgment (TOJ)** — the exact moment the chart is read or a question is asked.

| # | Ruling Planet | How Identified | Strength |
|---|---------------|----------------|---------|
| 1 | **Lagna Star Lord** | Nakshatra lord of the rising degree at TOJ | Strongest |
| 2 | **Lagna Sign Lord** | Sign ruler of the rising sign at TOJ | 2nd |
| 3 | **Moon Star Lord** | Nakshatra lord of Moon's position at TOJ | 3rd |
| 4 | **Moon Sign Lord** | Sign ruler of Moon's rashi at TOJ | 4th |
| 5 | **Day Lord** | Planet ruling the weekday | Weakest |

**Day Lord reference**: Sun=Sunday | Moon=Monday | Mars=Tuesday | Mercury=Wednesday | Jupiter=Thursday | Venus=Friday | Saturn=Saturday

## How to Use Ruling Planets

**Verification**: If a key natal significator also appears as a current Ruling Planet → prediction is confirmed with high confidence.

**Timing**: Events occur in Dasha/Bhukti/Antara of planets that are BOTH significators of relevant houses AND Ruling Planets at TOJ simultaneously.

**Retrograde caution**: "Ruling planets in a star or sub of a retrograde planet should be discarded." Retrograde RPs indicate delayed or reopened matters.

**Core principle**: The group of RPs at birth, at time of chart reading, and at the moment of the event are cosmically synchronized. When a significator is also a ruling planet, timing confidence is maximum.

# PART 9 — RETROGRADE PLANETS IN KP

## In Natal Charts

Retrograde planets still function via their star lord chain. However they tend to:
- Show internal, delayed, or revisited themes in the houses they occupy
- Deliver results that come with reversals or returning to previous states

## In Horary Charts (Critical Rules)

| Retrograde Status | Rule |
|-------------------|------|
| Cuspal sub-lord is retrograde | Event will occur AFTER the planet turns direct |
| Sub-lord's star lord is retrograde | **Event will NOT take place** (denial) |
| Sub-lord of the sub-lord is retrograde | Event will occur after THAT planet turns direct |

## For Ruling Planets

**Rule**: "Ruling planets in a star or sub of a retrograde planet should be discarded from the RP list."

A retrograde RP = delayed or reopened matter. Use to time the revival: event manifests when the planet goes direct.

# PART 11 — KP EVENT PREDICTION FRAMEWORK

2. **Dasha timing**: Current Dasha/Bhukti/Antara lords are significators of the relevant houses
3. **Ruling planet confirmation**: At least some of the Dasha lords are current Ruling Planets

## The Full Prediction Process

